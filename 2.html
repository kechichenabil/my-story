What is inguinal hernia?

An inguinal hernia occurs when part of the lining of the abdominal cavity (omentum) or intestine protrudes through a weak spot in the abdomen — usually along the inguinal canal, which carries the spermatic cord in men. An inguinal hernia isn't necessarily dangerous. However, it doesn't get better on its own, and it can lead to life-threatening complications if you don't have surgery.
